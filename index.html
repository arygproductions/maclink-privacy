Privacy Policy for MacLink

Last updated: 24 June 2026

MacLink ("the app") is a companion app that connects your Android phone to the MacLink desktop application running on your own Mac, over your own local Wi-Fi network. This policy explains what the app does and does not do with your information.

The short version

MacLink does not have user accounts, does not run ads, and does not use analytics or tracking. It does not send your data to us or to any third party. All communication happens directly between your phone and your own Mac on your local network.

Information the app handles

Mac connection address. When you connect to your Mac (by scanning a QR code or entering an address), the app stores that address locally on your phone so it can reconnect later. This is kept only on your device and is never transmitted to us.

Files you send and receive. When you send a file, the app reads the file you pick so it can transfer it to your Mac. When you receive a file, it is saved to the Downloads/MacLink folder on your phone. These files are transferred directly between your phone and your Mac on your local network. We never receive, store, or have access to them.

Camera. The app uses your phone's camera only to scan the QR code shown by the MacLink Mac app, so it can read your Mac's connection address. The camera is not used for any other purpose, and no images are stored or transmitted.

Network access. The app uses your local network connection to talk to your Mac. Because this is a direct connection on your own LAN, it uses standard HTTP rather than HTTPS. No data leaves your local network as part of normal use.

Information we collect

We (the developer) do not collect any personal information through this app. There is no server operated by us that your data passes through.

Data sharing

We do not share, sell, or transfer any user data, because we do not collect any.

Children's privacy

The app is a general-purpose file-transfer utility and is not directed at children under 13.

Permissions summary

  • Camera — to scan the QR code that contains your Mac's address.
  • Internet / network state — to communicate with your Mac over your local network.
